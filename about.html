About

Surrounded by a creative family, I’ve always been interested in art. After my sixth birthday my mother started to give me drawing lessons, simple things, like circles, cubes and pyramids. With the help of several drawing books, written by Christopher Hart, I became passionate about diverse drawing styles, going from observational drawing to manga and fantasy. When I was nine, I was finally old enough to join the Art Academy of Geraardsbergen. I took classes for 9 years, and graduated in 2015. I would have loved to go to an art school but started and graduated Science/Mathematics in KA Geraardsbergen where I developed a great interest in biology and math. Still, I knew I wanted to study Graphic Design, against the advice of my teachers who’d rather wanted me to study engineering. Living by the motto ‘Sometimes you have to do what you love to do, instead of something you are good at’, spoken by my math teacher, I decided to start my education at LUCA School of Art in Ghent. Some day, I hope to unite science and art in my work.
